1.  **Acquire** - Buy or obtain (an asset or object) for oneself; learn or develop (a skill, habit, or quality).  
    _"She managed to acquire the necessary skills for the job."_

2.  **Adapt** - Make (something) suitable for a new use or purpose; modify; become adjusted to new conditions.  
    _"We need to adapt our strategy to the changing market."_

3.  **Advocate** - Publicly recommend or support; a person who publicly supports or recommends a particular cause or policy.  
    _"He advocates for stricter environmental regulations."_

4.  **Aggregate** - Form or group into a class or cluster; a whole formed by combining several elements.  
    _"The report aggregates data from multiple sources."_

5.  **Aid** - Help, typically of a practical nature.  
    _"Foreign aid was sent to the disaster-stricken area."_

6.  **Alleviate** - Make (suffering, deficiency, or a problem) less severe.  
    _"The medication helped to alleviate her pain."_

7.  **Ambivalent** - Having mixed feelings or contradictory ideas about something or someone.  
    _"She felt ambivalent about leaving her job."_

8.  **Analyze** - Examine methodically and in detail the constitution or structure of (something, especially information), typically for purposes of explanation and interpretation.  
    _"The researchers analyzed the results of the experiment."_

9.  **Annual** - Occurring once every year.  
    _"The company holds an annual general meeting."_

10. **Appreciate** - Recognize the full worth of; understand (a situation) fully; recognize the implications of.  
    _"I really appreciate all the help you've given me."_

11. **Approach** - A way of dealing with something; come near or nearer to (someone or something) in distance or time.  
    _"We need a new approach to tackle this problem."_

12. **Appropriate** - Suitable or proper in the circumstances; take (something) for one's own use, typically without the owner's permission.  
    _"Please wear appropriate attire for the interview."_

13. **Approximate** - Close to the actual, but not completely accurate or exact.  
    _"The approximate cost will be around $500."_

14. **Articulate** - Having or showing the ability to speak fluently and coherently; express (an idea or feeling) fluently and coherently.  
    _"She gave a very articulate presentation."_

15. **Assess** - Evaluate or estimate the nature, ability, or quality of.  
    _"We need to assess the impact of the new policy."_

16. **Assign** - Allocate (a job or duty); designate or set (something) aside for a specific purpose.  
    _"Each student was assigned a mentor."_

17. **Assume** - Suppose to be the case, without proof; take or begin to have (power or responsibility).  
    _"I assume you'll be attending the meeting."_

18. **Attitude** - A settled way of thinking or feeling about something.  
    _"He has a very positive attitude towards his work."_

19. **Authorize** - Give official permission for or approval to (an undertaking or agent).  
    _"Only managers can authorize expenditure over $100."_

20. **Benefit** - An advantage or profit gained from something.  
    _"Regular exercise has many health benefits."_

21. **Capable** - Having the ability, fitness, or quality necessary to do or achieve a specified thing.  
    _"She is capable of handling complex tasks."_

22. **Capacity** - The maximum amount that something can contain; the ability or power to do or understand something.  
    _"The stadium has a seating capacity of 50,000."_

23. **Categorize** - Place in a particular class or group.  
    _"The books were categorized by genre."_

24. **Cite** - Quote (a passage, book, or author) as evidence for or justification of an argument or statement, especially in a scholarly work.  
    _"Remember to cite all your sources in the essay."_

25. **Clarify** - Make (a statement or situation) less confused and more comprehensible.  
    _"Could you clarify the last point you made?"_

26. **Collaborate** - Work jointly on an activity, especially to produce or create something.  
    _"The two companies collaborated on the project."_

27. **Commit** - Pledge or bind (a person or an organization) to a certain course or policy; carry out or perpetrate (a mistake, crime, or immoral act).  
    _"The government committed itself to reducing unemployment."_

28. **Commodity** - A raw material or primary agricultural product that can be bought and sold, such as copper or coffee; a useful or valuable thing.  
    _"Oil is a vital commodity in the global economy."_

29. **Compensate** - Give (someone) something, typically money, in recognition of loss, suffering, or injury incurred; recompense.  
    _"They compensated her for the damages."_

30. **Complex** - Consisting of many different and connected parts; not easy to analyze or understand.  
    _"The situation is more complex than it first appears."_

31. **Comply** - Act in accordance with a wish or command.  
    _"All businesses must comply with the regulations."_

32. **Concept** - An abstract idea; a general notion.  
    _"The concept of democracy varies around the world."_

33. **Conclude** - Bring (something) to an end; arrive at a judgment or opinion by reasoning.  
    _"The report concluded that the strategy was successful."_

34. **Conduct (noun)** - The manner in which a person behaves, especially in a particular place or situation.  
    _"His conduct during the meeting was unprofessional."_

35. **Confirm** - Establish the truth or correctness of (something previously believed or suspected to be the case).  
    _"Please confirm your attendance by Friday."_

36. **Consequent** - Following as a result or effect.  
    _"The rise in prices and consequent fall in demand."_

37. **Considerable** - Notably large in size, amount, or extent.  
    _"The project required a considerable amount of funding."_

38. **Consistent** - Acting or done in the same way over time, especially so as to be fair or accurate; compatible or in agreement with something.  
    _"Her work has shown consistent improvement."_

39. **Constitute** - Be (a part) of a whole; give legal or constitutional form to (an institution); establish by law.  
    _"Women constitute a large proportion of the workforce."_

40. **Consult** - Seek information or advice from (someone with expertise in a particular area).  
    _"You should consult a doctor if the symptoms persist."_

41. **Consume** - Eat, drink, or ingest (food or drink); use up (a resource).  
    _"The average household consumes a lot of energy."_

42. **Context** - The circumstances that form the setting for an event, statement, or idea, and in terms of which it can be fully understood.  
    _"You need to understand the historical context of the novel."_

43. **Contribute** - Give (something, especially money) in order to help achieve or provide something; help to cause or bring about.  
    _"Everyone was asked to contribute ideas."_

44. **Convene** - Come or bring together for a meeting or activity; assemble.  
    _"The committee will convene at 11 AM."_

45. **Coordinate** - Bring the different elements of (a complex activity or organization) into a harmonious or efficient relationship.  
    _"We need to coordinate our efforts to achieve the best results."_

46. **Correspond** - Have a close similarity; match or agree almost exactly; communicate by exchanging letters.  
    _"The figures correspond closely to our estimates."_

47. **Criteria** - Plural of criterion: a principle or standard by which something may be judged or decided.  
    _"Candidates must meet the specific criteria for the job."_

48. **Data** - Facts and statistics collected together for reference or analysis.  
    _"The research involved collecting large amounts of data."_

49. **Debate** - A formal discussion on a particular topic in a public meeting or legislative assembly, in which opposing arguments are put forward.  
    _"There has been much debate about the new tax laws."_

50. **Deduce** - Arrive at (a fact or a conclusion) by reasoning; draw as a logical conclusion.  
    _"We can deduce a lot from these figures."_

51. **Define** - State or describe exactly the nature, scope, or meaning of.  
    _"It's important to define the key terms used in your essay."_

52. **Demonstrate** - Clearly show the existence or truth of (something) by giving proof or evidence; give a practical exhibition and explanation of (how a machine, skill, or craft works or is performed).  
    _"The study demonstrates the link between smoking and cancer."_

53. **Derive** - Obtain something from (a specified source).  
    _"Many English words derive from Latin."_

54. **Designate** - Officially assign a specified status or purpose to.  
    _"This area has been designated as a national park."_

55. **Despite** - Without being affected by; in spite of.  
    _"He went for a walk despite the rain."_

56. **Diminish** - Make or become less.  
    _"Our enthusiasm gradually diminished."_

57. **Distinct** - Recognizably different in nature from something else of a similar type.  
    _"There are distinct differences between the two approaches."_

58. **Distribute** - Give shares of (something); deal out.  
    _"Food and clothing were distributed among the refugees."_

59. **Domestic** - Relating to the running of a home or to family relations; existing or occurring inside a particular country; not foreign or international.  
    _"The company focuses on the domestic market."_

60. **Dominant** - Most important, powerful, or influential.  
    _"The dominant culture often influences others."_

61. **Draft** - A preliminary version of a piece of writing; prepare a preliminary version of (a text).  
    _"He submitted the first draft of his report."_

62. **Duration** - The time during which something continues.  
    _"The course is of three years' duration."_

63. **Element** - A part or aspect of something abstract, especially one that is essential or characteristic.  
    _"Trust is a key element of any relationship."_

64. **Enable** - Give (someone or something) the authority or means to do something.  
    _"Technology enables us to work more efficiently."_

65. **Encounter** - Unexpectedly experience or be faced with (something difficult or hostile); meet (someone) unexpectedly.  
    _"We encountered several problems during the project."_

66. **Enforce** - Compel observance of or compliance with (a law, rule, or obligation).  
    _"The police are there to enforce the law."_

67. **Ensure** - Make certain that (something) shall occur or be the case.  
    _"Please ensure all lights are switched off."_

68. **Equivalent** - Equal in value, amount, function, meaning, etc.  
    _"The qualification is equivalent to a degree."_

69. **Estimate** - Roughly calculate or judge the value, number, quantity, or extent of.  
    _"They estimated the cost of the repairs."_

70. **Evident** - Plain or obvious; clearly seen or understood.  
    _"It was evident that she was disappointed."_

71. **Exclude** - Deny (someone) access to or bar (someone) from a place, group, or privilege.  
    _"Children under 16 are excluded from this event."_

72. **Exhibit** - Publicly display (a work of art or item of interest) in an art gallery or museum or at a trade fair; manifest or deliberately display (a quality or a type of behaviour).  
    _"The museum will exhibit artifacts from ancient Egypt."_

73. **Expand** - Become or make larger or more extensive.  
    _"The company plans to expand its operations overseas."_

74. **Expertise** - Expert skill or knowledge in a particular field.  
    _"We need someone with expertise in digital marketing."_

75. **Facilitate** - Make (an action or process) easy or easier.  
    _"The translator facilitated communication between the two leaders."_

76. **Factor** - A circumstance, fact, or influence that contributes to a result or outcome.  
    _"Cost is a major factor in our decision."_

77. **Feature** - A distinctive attribute or aspect of something; have as a prominent attribute or aspect.  
    _"The new smartphone features a larger screen."_

78. **Federal** - Relating to or denoting the central government as distinguished from the constituent units in a federation.  
    _"Federal law overrides state law in this case."_

79. **Framework** - A basic structure underlying a system, concept, or text.  
    _"We need to establish a clear framework for the project."_

80. **Function** - An activity or purpose natural to or intended for a person or thing; work or operate in a proper or particular way.  
    _"The function of the heart is to pump blood."_

81. **Generate** - Produce or create.  
    _"The wind turbines generate electricity."_

82. **Grant** - Agree to give or allow (something requested) to; agree or admit to (someone) that (something) is true.  
    _"The university granted her a scholarship."_

83. **Hence** - As a consequence; for this reason.  
    _"The roads were covered in ice; hence, it was not safe to drive."_

84. **Hypothesis** - A supposition or proposed explanation made on the basis of limited evidence as a starting point for further investigation.  
    _"The results support our initial hypothesis."_

85. **Identify** - Establish or indicate who or what (someone or something) is.  
    _"She was able to identify the main problems."_

86. **Illustrate** - Provide (a book, newspaper, etc.) with pictures; serve as an example of.  
    _"The data illustrates the current economic trend."_

87. **Impact** - The action of one object coming forcibly into contact with another; have a strong effect on someone or something.  
    _"The new regulations will impact small businesses."_

88. **Implement** - Put (a decision, plan, agreement, etc.) into effect.  
    _"The company will implement the new policy next month."_

89. **Implicate** - Show (someone) to be involved in a crime; convey (a meaning or intention) indirectly through what one says, rather than stating it explicitly.  
    _"The evidence implicated him in the robbery."_

90. **Impose** - Force (something unwelcome or unfamiliar) to be accepted or put in place; take advantage of someone by demanding their attention or commitment.  
    _"The government imposed new taxes."_

91. **Index** - An alphabetical list of names, subjects, etc., with references to the places where they occur, typically found at the end of a book; a sign or measure of something.  
    _"The consumer price index measures inflation."_

92. **Indicate** - Point out; show; suggest as a desirable or necessary course of action.  
    _"The survey results indicate a shift in public opinion."_

93. **Initial** - Existing or occurring at the beginning.  
    _"My initial reaction was one of surprise."_

94. **Instance** - An example or single occurrence of something.  
    _"There were several instances of misconduct."_

95. **Institute** - Introduce or establish (a scheme, undertaking, or policy); a society or organization having a particular object or common factor, especially a scientific, educational, or social one.  
    _"They instituted new safety procedures."_

96. **Interact** - Act in such a way as to have an effect on another; act reciprocally.  
    _"Customers can interact with the company via social media."_

97. **Interpret** - Explain the meaning of (information, words, or actions); understand (an action, mood, or way of behaving) as having a particular meaning or significance.  
    _"It's difficult to interpret these results."_

98. **Invest** - Expend money with the expectation of achieving a profit or material result by putting it into financial schemes, shares, or property, or by using it to develop a commercial venture.  
    _"He invested his savings in the stock market."_

99. **Justify** - Show or prove to be right or reasonable.  
    _"You need to justify your decision."_

100.  **Legislate** - Make or enact laws.  
      _"The government promised to legislate against discrimination."_
